**Here's a practical, in-depth guide to making custom refrigerator magnets in Amsterdam with your own images (MIT license, screenshots, Rock Street visuals, QR code, etc.).**

I'll break it down by your budget tiers (€10, €25, €50, €100). All options assume you're designing the artwork yourself (on your phone/laptop) with a clean layout: front = strong image, back = text + MIT license + DOI + QR code.

### €10 Budget (Ultra-cheap DIY, ~10–20 small magnets)
- Buy **magnetic sheets** (A4 self-adhesive or flexible magnetic paper) from a shop like **Action**, **Hema**, **Gamma**, or **Hornbach**.
- Print your designs at a cheap copy shop (e.g. **Copy.nl**, **Print&Go**, or library printers) on normal paper.
- Cut with scissors.
- **Expected output**: 10–15 small (4x4cm or 5x5cm) magnets.
- **Pros**: Cheapest, fast.
- **Cons**: Thinner magnets, lower durability/print quality.

### €25 Budget (Decent quality, ~20–30 magnets)
- Better magnetic sheets (thicker, stronger hold) + higher-quality photo paper or matte sticker paper.
- Print at a better copy shop with color calibration (e.g. **Staples** or independent print shops in Amsterdam Noord or Centrum).
- Laminate optionally for protection (€5 extra).
- **Expected output**: 20–30 nice 5x5cm or 6x8cm magnets.
- Good balance for distribution.

### €50 Budget (Solid professional look, 40–60 magnets)
- High-quality printable magnetic sheets or pre-made magnet blanks.
- Professional printing on photo paper or vinyl + laminate.
- Visit a dedicated print shop (ask for “magnet printing” or “magnetische folie”).
- Possible small run at a local sign shop.
- **Expected output**: 40–60 magnets with good strength and finish.
- Strong enough for Blender hand-outs and street distribution.

### €100 Budget (Best quality / higher quantity, 80–150+ magnets)
- Premium magnetic material + professional printing service.
- Use a local printer that does custom promotional magnets (vinyl or proper fridge magnet stock).
- Add QR code precision and nice rounding of edges.
- **Expected output**: 80–150+ high-quality magnets (various sizes possible).
- Leaves room for multiple designs (e.g. different visuals per magnet).

**Practical tips for Amsterdam**:
- Design first: Use Canva (free) for quick layouts optimized for small size.
- Search for “magnetische folie” or “koelkastmagneten drukken” locally.
- Many copy shops can do small runs same-day.
- Test one magnet first to check print quality and magnetic strength.
