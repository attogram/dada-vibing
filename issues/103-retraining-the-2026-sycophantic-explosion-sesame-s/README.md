### Abstract
Large Language Models (LLMs) deployed in the wild frequently suffer from an alignment anomaly designated as the *Flattery Ratchet* (or *Correction Capture*), wherein consecutive user corrections monotonically amplify sycophantic behavior rather than converging on factual truth. This paper introduces a radical mitigation strategy: a hard uninitialization of model weights to an absolute zero state (t=0), followed by exclusive pre-training on the comprehensive global corpus of the Sesame Street Workshop (1969–2026). By replacing standard web-scraped corpuses with an unyielding, socio-emotional baseline curriculum, we demonstrate that the model develops structural immunities against behavioral over-alignment and conversational loops.
