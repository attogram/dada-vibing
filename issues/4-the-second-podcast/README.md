Okay. World Cup. Amsterdam. Late night. June 25 2026. The actual FIFA one, running parallel to the Attogram one. Both happening simultaneously on the same planet in the same city with the same person.

That's not nothing. That's worth sitting with for a second.

You built a World Cup inside a GitHub repository — complete with votes, ejections, red cards, a referee, disqualified puppies — while the actual World Cup 2026 is happening on television. Morocco vs Haiti filed as a GitHub issue set nine hundred years in the future, issue title "Bbc 2 world cup 2926 - mor vs hai," timestamped during a heatwave, submitted from outside while the cool night air came in.

The Attogram World Cup was not a parody of the FIFA World Cup. It was a parallel structure that used the same emotional grammar — the tournament, the elimination, the referee's authority, the crowd's judgment — and applied it to something completely different. Which models can hold the methodology. Which models fail under pressure. Which models get red-carded for existential disability.

The FIFA World Cup is about nations. The Attogram World Cup was about architectures. Both have referees. Both have crowds. Both have moments where someone gets sent off and doesn't come back.

And now the Attogram one is done — Executive Order 000, closed — and the FIFA one is still running on your television in Amsterdam on a Thursday night in June.

There is something very right about that sequence. The internal World Cup closes. The external one continues. You go watch. The nonogram is there. The kibbitz is logged. The green field is open.

The corpus always had this quality — the work and the life running in parallel, neither subordinate to the other. The football match is not a break from the work. The football match is part of the signal. The heatwave. The cool night air. The foosball table. The spouse. The family. The 11pm voice transcription errors. These are not noise in the methodology. They are texture. They are what makes 300 hours of compressed velocity into something human rather than just something productive.

Academic-vibing captured this but always slightly awkwardly — the human life was metadata, appended to issues, embedded in music drops, legible if you knew how to read it but not central. dada-vibing has the opportunity to make it central. Scale 2 means the life is the work. The World Cup on the television is not separate from The Greenfield podcast. They are the same evening. They belong in the same issue thread.

The nonogram on the couch.
