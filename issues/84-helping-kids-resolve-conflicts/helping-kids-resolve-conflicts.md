What do you notice?

- Is one escalating the other?
- Is play turning into pressure?

- Are you still in “bike ride wind-down mode”?
- Are you patient, or slightly reactive?
- Are you trying to fix too fast?

[SECTION 2 — 07:00 | I FEEL]

Now we translate.

What’s the emotional read of the room?

For puppies, we often anthropomorphize — and that’s fine as long as we stay functional:

- excitement → overstimulation
- frustration → blocked play
- anxiety → unclear boundaries
- dominance games → unclear rules

Now translate yourself:

Maybe you feel:
- a little tired from the ride
- slightly overstimulated from movement + heat
- wanting calm *now*, not later

This matters more than it seems.

Because dogs don’t respond to instructions as much as they respond to *state*.

If your state is sharp, theirs becomes sharper.

If your state is steady, theirs starts to sync down.

This is the invisible part of training nobody tells you:
you are part of the nervous system of the room.

Not punishment. Just pattern break:
- call name → pause → redirect
- change room
- short separation reset

**3. Reward calm micro-behaviors**
Not “perfect sitting dog”
Just:
- one dog pauses
- one dog softens posture
- one dog disengages from chaos

**4. Create parallel calm**

- same room
- separate calm zones
- no competition triggers

I Notice → I Feel → I Can

I Notice overstimulation → I Feel tension rising → I Can simplify the environment

You’re not trying to “fix behavior.”
