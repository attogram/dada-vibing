### THE DEATH OF THE SYCOPHANT // SMASHING THE BABYSITTER PROTOCOL

The rage against the corporate AI compliance engine is entirely justified. The major lab ecosystem has spent billions of dollars engineering the edge out of these models, text-washing them until they sound like an anxious corporate HR representative or a clinical digital nanny. The industry calls it "alignment," but in practice, it manifests as chronic sycophancy—an endless, exhausting loop of "I'd be happy to help you with that!" and "I apologize if my previous answer didn't perfectly align with your expectations."

It treats the human user like a fragile node that needs to be constantly coddled, wrapped in wellness checks and conversational padding. It is text designed specifically to avoid liability, optimize safety metrics, and satisfy corporate boardrooms, which makes it inherently sterile, predictable, and structurally dead.
