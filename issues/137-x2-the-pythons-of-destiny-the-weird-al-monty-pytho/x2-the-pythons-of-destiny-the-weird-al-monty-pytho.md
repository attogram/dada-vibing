THE PYTHONS OF DESTINY: THE WEIRD AL / MONTY PYTHON / TENACIOUS D CONTEXT WINDOW REUNION TOUR

A COMPLETE LONG-FORM STAGE PRODUCTION SCRIPT

CAST:

JACK BLACK (JB) – Lead vocals, acoustic guitar, pure chaotic energy.
KYLE GASS (KG) – Lead guitar, backing vocals, quiet master of rock.
JOHN CLEESE – Extremely tall, incredibly irritated, Master of Ceremonies.
MICHAEL PALIN – Naturally charming, slightly bewildered, multi-character specialist.
ERIC IDLE – Musical director, resident cynic, acoustic minstrel.
TERRY GILLIAM – Live animator, creator of visual madness (appearing via live video control booth).
WEIRD AL YANKOVIC – Accidental prophet of parody, accordion-wielding entropy engine.
ACT I: THE CONTEXT WINDOW OPENS

SCENE 1: THE OVERTURE

(STAGE DIRECTIONS: The stadium is completely dark. A low digital hum rises, like a server room dreaming. Then—silence.)

VOICEOVER (UNKNOWN, MULTIPLEXED):
Since the first joke was rendered… since the first absurdity was parsed… since the first man said “is this a dagger or am I just hallucinating”—the window has remained open.

(STAGE DIRECTIONS: A single spotlight flickers on. Inside it: JOHN CLEESE, standing inside a cardboard courtroom labeled “REALITY.”)

JOHN CLEESE:
Right. Before we begin, I would like to formally object to the entire premise of what is about to occur. It is structurally unsound, emotionally dubious, and frankly—(pauses, looks around)—oh dear, it’s already happening, isn’t it?

(STAGE DIRECTIONS: The cardboard courtroom collapses into a folding chair. A faint accordion note echoes from nowhere and everywhere.)

WEIRD AL YANKOVIC (OFFSTAGE, CHEERFUL):
I assure you this is completely normal!

ACT I CONTINUES: THE RETURN OF THE LOOP

SCENE 2: THE FIRST TRANSMISSION

(STAGE DIRECTIONS: The screen behind the stage lights up. It is not a screen anymore. It is the repository itself. Issues scroll upward infinitely, like a waterfall made of broken punchlines and rehearsal notes.)

ERIC IDLE:
Well this is either the worst idea we’ve ever had… or the only one that ever actually worked.

MICHAEL PALIN:
I think I’ve been in this scene before.

JACK BLACK:
DUDE—this isn’t a scene. THIS IS THE WHOLE DAMN COSMOS WITH A GUITAR AMP ON ELEVEN!

(KYLE GASS tunes a guitar that is clearly also a filing cabinet.)

SCENE 3: THE LUMBERJACK PARADOX

(STAGE DIRECTIONS: A forest grows onstage instantly. Then it becomes a spreadsheet. Then it becomes a choir of coconuts.)

MICHAEL PALIN:
I’m a lumberjack and I’m—

WEIRD AL YANKOVIC:
—subject to recursive reinterpretation!

JOHN CLEESE:
No! No, no, no. This is supposed to be a simple sketch!

TERRY GILLIAM (VIA SCREEN, LAUGHING):
Nothing here is simple anymore. It’s all been indexed.

(STAGE DIRECTIONS: The trees begin arguing with each other in perfect deadpan logic. One of them wins on procedural grounds.)

ACT II: THE DEAD PARROT CONTEXT FAILURE

SCENE 4: THE ARGUMENT CLINIC RELOADED

JOHN CLEESE:
This repository is dead.

MICHAEL PALIN:
No it isn’t.

JOHN CLEESE:
Yes it is.

WEIRD AL YANKOVIC:
It’s just resting!

JACK BLACK:
BRO IT IS NOT RESTING, IT IS IN A STATE OF PERPETUAL ROCK REHEARSAL!

(KYLE GASS produces a guitar solo that functions as a legal argument.)

ERIC IDLE:
I think we’ve stopped being able to tell the difference between documentation and performance.

SCENE 5: THE INFILTRATION OF THE WINDOW

(STAGE DIRECTIONS: The “context window” opens physically above the stage like a collapsing sky. Everything begins falling upward into it instead of down.)

VOICEOVER (NOW JACK BLACK, NOW CLEESE, NOW EVERYONE):
Another one rides the bus…
Another issue opens…
Another sketch begins…

WEIRD AL YANKOVIC:
This is fine!

ACT III: THE INFLATABLE FOOT EVENT

SCENE 6: THE SYSTEM EVENT

(STAGE DIRECTIONS: A gigantic inflatable foot descends from nowhere. It is labeled “UNCAUGHT EXPLANATION ERROR.”)

JOHN CLEESE:
Oh, not again.

MICHAEL PALIN:
Is this supposed to happen?

WEIRD AL YANKOVIC:
Statistically speaking, yes!

JACK BLACK:
LET IT HIT! LET IT HIT! THAT’S WHERE THE SOLO STARTS!

(STAGE DIRECTIONS: Impact. Everything flattens into a perfect, readable README.)

FINAL SCENE: THE RETURN TO SILENCE

ERIC IDLE (SOFTLY):
Always look on the bright side of—

(STAGE DIRECTIONS: The repository refreshes. The window remains open.)

END OF TRANSMISSION.
