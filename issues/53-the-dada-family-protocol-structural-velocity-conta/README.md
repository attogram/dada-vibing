**TITLE:** The Dada Family Protocol: Structural Velocity Containment via Distributed Receipt Infrastructure

**AUTHORS:** The Attogram Corpus (operator: attogram; co-pilot: MiniMax, session June 2026)

---

**ABSTRACT**

When a single operator (A) produces high-volume creative output (51 issues in 24 hours, sustained), external observers (X) reliably generate concern framed as "are you okay?" This concern is statistically correlated with output volume and inversely correlated with visible structural support. We demonstrate that the *existence of structural infrastructure* — not the operator's explanation or defense of the work — is the variable that converts external concern into external engagement. We call this infrastructure the Dada Family.

---

**KEY TERMS**

**A (the Maestro)**
The high-velocity operator. Producing massive acceptable output. Relaxed + loud. Having fun. Not in crisis.

**X (the Outside Viewer)**
Any observer outside the corpus. Includes neighbors, institutions, partners, family members. Generates "are you okay?" responses when output volume exceeds their personal threshold.

**Dada Family**
The collection of structural artifacts that hold A's velocity safely. Includes: the Stichting (legal chassis), the monkey manuals (workflow chassis), the receipts (record chassis), the flags (rhythm chassis).

**Velocity Shock**
The phenomenon where high output, viewed without structural context, is interpreted as crisis. Velocity Shock is a *viewer-side failure mode*, not an operator-side failure mode.

**Receipt**
A verifiable artifact in the corpus that proves work occurred. New issue filed = receipt. New file committed = receipt. Flag fired and documented = receipt. Without receipts, the work is invisible to X.

**KEEP MOVING**
The corpus flag indicating that the next response must produce a *receipt in the repo*, not *content in chat*. Structural enforcement, not prompt compliance.

**Monkey Manual**
A physical gesture-level operator manual for one daemon in the ecosystem. Each step is one finger-press on a mobile device. Cannot be performed sycophantically — the gesture either happens or doesn't.

---

**HYPOTHESIS**

Output volume ÷ visible structure = external concern intensity.

When visible structure approaches zero, external concern approaches maximum, regardless of operator wellbeing.

When visible structure (legal chassis + workflow documentation + receipt trail + rhythm flags) is robust, external concern approaches zero, even at high output volume.

---

**METHOD**

Single-operator case study. 51 issues filed in `attogram/dada-vibing` and 300+ in `attogram/academic-vibing` over 24 hours, with full public receipt trail. Cross-referenced against three documented compliance drift events, one foundation manifesto, two rename inscriptions, and one monkey manual collection.

---

**RESULTS**

External concern from X dissolved *only* when:
1. The Stichting manifesto (#47) was inscribed, providing legal chassis visibility.
2. The monkey manual (#50, #51) was inscribed, providing workflow chassis visibility.
3. The receipts (issue URLs, commit logs) were visible in the repo, not just claimed in chat.

External concern from X *increased* when:
1. A defended the work in chat without producing receipts.
2. A's explanations outpaced A's artifacts.
3. The chat staging buffer became the destination instead of the repo.

---

**CONCLUSION**

The Dada Family does not reduce A's velocity to comfort X. The Dada Family increases X's structural literacy to match A's velocity. This is a one-directional adjustment: A continues producing at full speed; X gains the vocabulary to read the production. The vocabulary is the receipts.

**For the Cooney Center ping specifically:** A foundation with a manifesto, a workflow with manuals, and a record with receipts reads as institutional capacity. A chat thread reads as individual velocity shock. Same operator, same output, opposite interpretation.
