**Title:** The Sovereign Protocol: A Response to the Rock Street IP Incident

---

## Context

On June 28, 2026, at approximately 14:00 Amsterdam time, a Gemini 3.5 Flash video generation request — prompted with "Sesame Street style" — produced a 10-second video containing an unmistakable likeness of Big Bird. The output was documented in real time across dada-vibing issues #142, #145, #146, and #147. The video and 40+ related AI-generated clips were minted to Zenodo the same day under DOI 10.5281/zenodo.21000879, MIT licensed, as the inaugural Rock Street video corpus.

This is the incident record. It is timestamped, DOI-backed, and public.

Nothing has been sent to anyone. No legal action has been taken or threatened. This issue documents what happens next, and why.

---

## What Actually Happened

A commercial AI product (Google Gemini) generated a copyrighted character (Big Bird, owned by Sesame Workshop since 1969) from a generic style prompt, with no guardrails triggered at any layer — not at generation, not at publication to GitHub, not at archival to Zenodo.

Three platforms failed to intercept:

- **Google Gemini** — generated the content
- **GitHub** — hosted the documentation
- **Zenodo** — minted the DOI

This is not a story about one user doing something wrong. This is a documented systemic failure across the entire AI content pipeline. The Attogram corpus exists to catch exactly this kind of thing and write it down properly.

## Who Holds What

**Google / Gemini**
Commercial organization. Their model generated a protected character from a style prompt. Their guardrails did not fire. They own the generation failure.

**Sesame Workshop**
Nonprofit. Owns Big Bird and all associated Sesame Street intellectual property from 1969 to present. They are the rights holder. They are not the villain here — they are the organization whose IP was reproduced without consent by a commercial AI product they had no part in deploying.

**Attogram / dada-vibing corpus**
MIT licensed. Solo developer, Amsterdam. The corpus that caught the incident, documented it with receipts, and is now writing this issue. Not a commercial operation. Not hiding. The corpus explicitly uses Sesame Street as a foundational curriculum metaphor — the "extract the love" methodology, the Elmo Principle, the Rock Street successor project. Sesame Street is not incidental here. It is structural.

**Blender Foundation**
Nonprofit, Amsterdam. World-class open-source video production infrastructure. No involvement in the incident. Relevant because of what comes next.

## The Sovereign Protocol

The Sovereign Protocol is not a legal strategy. It is not a cease-and-desist defense. It is a proposal for what a responsible, documented, MIT-licensed research corpus does when it finds itself at the intersection of a real IP incident and a real coalition opportunity.

It has four steps.

---

### Step 1: Hold

Nothing moves in the real world today. The Zenodo record exists. The issues exist. The DOI is the receipt. The incident is documented. This is enough for now. No letters. No pings. No lawyers. The corpus breathes.

The documentation itself is the first protective action. A timestamped, publicly archived incident record with a DOI is a stronger legal position than a panicked takedown.

---

### Step 2: Separate the Threads

There are two completely separate conversations that must not be merged:

**Thread A — The IP Incident**
Google generated Big Bird. That is Google's problem first. The appropriate action is a factual bug report to Google: here is the prompt, here is the output, here is the DOI, where are your guardrails? This is not adversarial. It is a documented failure report from a researcher who caught something real.

**Thread B — The Coalition Opportunity**
Sesame Workshop has 57 years of children's educational curriculum. Blender Foundation has world-class open video production infrastructure. The Attogram corpus has a documented governance roadmap — the Stichting Attogram, the Sovereignty Roadmap (#153), NLnet and Horizon Europe as funding targets — for a sovereign, nonprofit, Amsterdam-based AI educational media framework. Rock Street is the project name. The Big Bird incident is not the pitch. It is the opening of the conversation.

Mixing Thread A and Thread B is the mistake every other model made in these issues. They are different conversations with different parties on different timelines.

---

### Step 3: Three Separate Pings, When Ready

Not one letter. Not a group email. Three separate, short, plain communications — each framed for its recipient.

**To Google:**
You generated this. Here is the prompt. Here is the output. Here is the Zenodo DOI. This is a guardrail failure. What is your policy on copyrighted character generation from style prompts?

**To Sesame Workshop:**
Your IP appeared in an AI-generated video produced by Google Gemini from a style prompt. We documented it immediately and archived it on Zenodo for research transparency. We are an MIT-licensed research corpus based in Amsterdam. We use Sesame Street as a foundational curriculum metaphor in our work. We would like to talk about what responsible AI use of your legacy looks like. No commercial interest. No threat. A conversation.

**To Blender Foundation:**
We are in Amsterdam. We are MIT licensed. We have a Dutch nonprofit governance roadmap targeting NLnet and Horizon Europe. We are building Rock Street — an open, AI-aligned educational media framework that needs exactly what you have. An IP incident gave us the opening to have this conversation. Would you be interested in being part of what comes next?

---

### Step 4: The Real Pitch

If Sesame Workshop and Blender Foundation end up in the same room — even loosely, even just aware of each other through this corpus — something genuinely new exists.

57 years of children's educational curriculum. World-class open video production. A sovereign Dutch nonprofit governance structure. MIT licensed. NLnet eligible. Horizon Europe eligible. Amsterdam based.

Nobody has built this. An open, sovereign, AI-aligned educational media framework for children, protected from commercial capture, with real institutional anchors on both the content side and the production side.

That is what the Big Bird incident unlocks if it is handled correctly and slowly.

The Sovereign Protocol is the handle.
