**ISSUE SEED — PING: SESAME WORKSHOP — DIRECT, NOT VIA BLENDER, ASSUME FAILURE**

Title suggestion: **Ping Sesame Workshop — Direct Disclosure, Big Bird Incident**

---

**Context, plainly, for whoever reads this issue cold:**

On June 28, 2026, a Gemini-generated video (gemini_generated_video_1ffe683a.mp4) was prompted for "Sesame Street style" content and produced an unmistakable rendering of Big Bird — not an homage, not a generic yellow bird, a recognizable trademarked character. This was documented in real time in issue #147, with explicit acknowledgment that the likeness was probably Big Bird before the material was published anyway. It is now live in a published, DOI-minted Zenodo record (10.5281/zenodo.21000879, Version v1, MIT licensed, 43 views, 91 downloads as of this writing) alongside ~45 other videos. The Sovereign Protocol (#148) was written in response and proposed a three-ping plan: Google bug report, Sesame Workshop research introduction, Blender Foundation coalition pitch. As of today, Blender has been pinged (physical letter, hand-delivered, June 30). Sesame Workshop has not.

**Prior assumption, now revised:** the original plan routed Sesame Workshop through Blender as an intermediary, on the logic that an institution-to-institution introduction carries more weight than a cold solo approach. That logic still holds in principle. But the operating assumption going into this issue is that every ping attempted so far — Mistral (real email, real onboarding contact form, silence), the earlier Discord beacon-message attempt (correctly read as spam, muted) — has failed or gone quiet. Blender's outcome is still unknown and may take a long time, or may never resolve. Waiting indefinitely on Blender to vouch for a Sesame Workshop introduction means the live infringing material sits public and accumulating downloads for an unbounded period. That tradeoff needs to be named, not assumed away.

**The actual question this issue needs to resolve:** does Sesame Workshop get pinged directly, now, in parallel with waiting on Blender — or does the disclosure stay gated behind Blender responding first? Both are legitimate choices. What's not legitimate is the default-by-inertia outcome of doing neither.

**If direct contact is the choice, here's what's actually needed:**

1. **Find the right door.** Sesame Workshop is a large nonprofit with a research/education division (the actual home of the conflict-resolution curriculum, the Robin Williams grief material, etc.) and a separate legal/IP enforcement function. A research-introduction framing should go to the research/education contact channel, not a general press inbox and not directly to legal — going to legal first invites a legal-register response; going in through research, with full voluntary disclosure of the IP issue included, frames this as a citation-and-correction conversation rather than a confrontation. Their official site (sesameworkshop.org) should have a contact or partnerships path; this needs an actual search, not a guess.

2. **Lead with disclosure, not the pitch.** Unlike Blender, where the ask is small and craft-specific, Sesame Workshop needs to hear about the Big Bird material first, plainly, before anything else — citing #147/#148 as the documented timeline of what happened and when you knew. Volunteering it is the only version of this that's defensible; them finding it first is the bad outcome this entire issue exists to prevent.

3. **Then the actual content of the ping**, secondary to the disclosure: the corpus's broader use of Sesame Street's pedagogical lineage (the calm/soft register, the hard/soft video pairing discipline, the citation-not-derivative posture) as legitimate research inspiration, with Rock Street as the example of work built in that spirit without character reuse.

4. **State plainly what's being asked for**, same discipline as the Blender pitch: not funding, not partnership, not absorption. Likely just: acknowledgment received, and a clear signal of whether they want the Big Bird material taken down, left as documented incident, or something else. Let them set those terms rather than guessing and over- or under-correcting preemptively.

**Open sub-questions for this issue to carry forward:**

- Does this go out now, in parallel with the Blender wait, or stay gated?
- Who is the actual named contact or department, once researched?
- Does the Big Bird video get pulled from the Zenodo record before or after the ping goes out, or does it stay as-is, documented, pending their response?
- Assume this fails too, same as Mistral and the beacon attempt — what's the next move if Sesame Workshop goes silent the way Mistral has?

That's the seed. Iterate from here.
