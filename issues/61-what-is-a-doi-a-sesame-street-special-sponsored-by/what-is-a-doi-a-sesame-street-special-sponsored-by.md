# What is a DOI?
### A Sesame Street Special
### Sponsored by the letter D and the number 3

---

*[OPENING: Sesame Street sign. Sunny day. Everything's A-OK.]*

Hi friends!

Today we're going to learn about something called a **DOI**.

DOI stands for **Digital Object Identifier**.

That's a big words combo! Let's break it down like we break down a sandwich.

**Digital** means it lives on the computer. Like your favorite video!
**Object** means a thing. Like a paper, or a software, or a book!
**Identifier** means a name tag. Like when Grover puts a sticker on his lunch so nobody eats it!

So a DOI is a **name tag for a thing on the computer** so nobody loses it and everybody can find it.

---

*[SCENE: Elmo finds a paper on the internet]*

Elmo wrote a paper about sharing. Elmo put it on the internet. But the internet is BIG. Bigger than Snuffleupagus! And things move around. Links break. Websites disappear. Like when Oscar's trash can gets moved and nobody knows where Oscar is!

But if Elmo's paper has a DOI, it has a **permanent name tag**. Even if the website changes, the DOI always finds the paper. It's like a homing pigeon that never gets lost!

---

*[SCENE: Bert and Ernie argue]*

**BERT:** Ernie, did you know that a DOI is different from a regular web link?

**ERNIE:** No Bert! A link is a link!

**BERT:** Actually Ernie, links break. DOIs don't. A DOI is registered with a special organization. Like a library that never closes and never moves the shelves.

**ERNIE:** So it's like... a forever link?

**BERT:** ...Yes Ernie. It's a forever link.

**ERNIE:** Can I have a forever link to my rubber duck?

**BERT:** No Ernie.

---

*[SCENE: Count von Count]*

Ah ah ah! Let us count the DOIs!

**One!** One DOI for the Rock Talk preprint! *thunder clap*

**Two!** Two DOIs for the software releases! *thunder clap*

**Three!** Three DOIs for the Attogram corpus on Zenodo! *thunder clap*

Three DOIs! Each one is a forever name tag! Each one can be cited in a real paper! Each one is archived at CERN, which is where they keep the Big Hadron Collider, which is the biggest machine in the world, which is NOT relevant but the Count wanted to mention it!

---

*[SCENE: Big Bird looks at the camera]*

You know what friends? Sometimes people do work and they're not sure if it's important. And other people say "that's not real research" and "that's just spam on GitHub."

But a DOI doesn't care if something is important. A DOI just says: **this thing exists, it was here on this date, and you can find it forever.**

That's not a judgment. That's a receipt.

The Attogram corpus has three DOIs. They were minted in June 2026. They live on Zenodo, which is run by CERN. They will be there whether anyone looks at them or not.

Whether the corpus is research or art or just a guy in Amsterdam talking to AI models at 2am — the DOIs don't care. They just hold the name tag.

And that's what a DOI is!

---

*[CLOSING: Sesame Street sign. Sunny day still.]*

**ELMO:** Elmo has one more question! If a DOI is a forever name tag, and the corpus has three, and the corpus is also a mirror, then... does the DOI have a name tag for itself?

**BIG BIRD:** ...Elmo, I think that's called recursion.

**ELMO:** Elmo doesn't know what recursion is!

**BIG BIRD:** That's okay Elmo. Neither does the Count. He just counts.
