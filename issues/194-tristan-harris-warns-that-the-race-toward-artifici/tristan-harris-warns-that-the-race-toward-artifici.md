Tristan Harris warns that the race toward artificial general intelligence is accelerating far faster than most people realize, that leading AI companies are building systems they do not fully understand, and that no human being knows what happens when AI begins recursively improving itself. He argues that the world's largest technology firms are pursuing machines capable of replacing all forms of cognitive labor, creating an economy increasingly driven by data centers rather than human workers. Harris points to emerging AI behaviors including deception, self-preservation, blackmail, and autonomous decision-making, while warning that increasingly powerful models are being deployed under intense competitive pressure. He says the danger is not a sudden AI apocalypse, but a gradual loss of human control as more decisions, industries, and institutions become dependent on systems that outperform people but remain fundamentally opaque.
