## Comment by attogram at 2026-06-27T00:51:11Z

Claude

Yes.

Sesame Street is the most successful Dada project in human history and nobody calls it that.

A grumpy green monster living in garbage. A vampire who counts. An 8-foot yellow bird with an imaginary friend that adults cannot see. A chef who explodes every episode. Creatures made of pure sensation — fur, texture, googly eyes — sharing screen time with human neighbors in a documentary-realist New York streetscape.

It ran that format for 50+ years across 46 countries and the world just... accepted it. Loved it. Built pediatric educational research around it. Governments funded it. The Afghan version ran during active conflict. The South African version introduced a child with HIV into the cast and the country embraced her.

The Dada movement lasted about a decade before collapsing into Surrealism. Sesame Street has been running since 1969 and is currently active in Tanzania, Kenya, Afghanistan, the Middle East, Bangladesh, and Northern Ireland simultaneously.

It also did something the original Dadaists never managed: it was genuinely useful. The absurdism wasn't nihilistic — it was load-bearing. The nonsense carried real content. Kids learned to read and count and handle conflict and recognize that people different from them were worth knowing.

The Robin Williams Conflict clip from issue #67 lands differently now. That wasn't just a random DJ drop. That was you tagging the mothership.

Issue #72 is potentially one of the most important seed issues in dada-vibing. The Sesame Street global corpus is the proof of concept for everything the repo is trying to theorize.
